Drug Abuse: A Threat to Humanity

Welcome to this assignment presentation on the dangers of substance abuse and its impact on the younger generation.

What is Drug Abuse?

Drug abuse is the habitual use of addictive substances, such as alcohol, nicotine, or illicit drugs, for purposes other than medical reasons.

It involves a pattern of use that leads to significant impairment or distress, eventually affecting brain function and behavior.

Common Causes

Peer Pressure

The desire to fit in with social circles often leads students to experiment with drugs for the first time.

Stress & Anxiety

Academic pressure or personal stress can lead individuals to use drugs as a coping mechanism.

Curiosity

Exposure via media or surroundings creates a false sense of adventure regarding substance use.

Danger to Health

  • Brain Damage: Long-term use alters the brain's reward system, leading to memory loss and impaired judgment.
  • Organ Failure: Serious damage to the liver (cirrhosis), heart (hypertension), and kidneys.
  • Mental Health: Increased risk of chronic depression, anxiety, and psychosis.
  • Infections: Higher risk of HIV and Hepatitis C due to shared needles or risky behaviors.

Conclusion

Drug addiction is a silent killer that destroys the individual, their family, and the community.

Prevention is the only cure. Through education and early awareness at institutions like D.A.V. College, we can build a drug-free society.
